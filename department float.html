Computer science

computer science is the study of computation, information, and automation Computer science spans theoretical disciplines (such as algorithms, theory of computation, and information theory) to applied disciplines including the design and implementation of hardware and software

Engineering

Engineering is the practice of using natural science, mathematics, and the engineering design process to solve problems within technology, increase efficiency and productivity, and improve systems. Modern engineering comprises many subfields which include designing and improving infrastructure, machinery, vehicles, electronics, materials, and energy systems

Science

Department of Science & Technology (DST), which promotes scientific activity in a country, or an academic department within a university that teaches various scientific subjects like physics, chemistry, biology, or computer science. The term also can refer to any of the diverse fields or branches of natural and social sciences, such as geology, environmental science, or psychology.

Mathematics

Mathematics is a field of study that discovers and organizes methods, theories and theorems that are developed and proved for the needs of empirical sciences and mathematics itself. There are many areas of mathematics, which include number theory (the study of numbers), algebra (the study of formulas and related structures), geometry (the study of shapes and spaces that contain them), analysis (the study of continuous changes), and set theory presently used as a foundation for all mathematics
